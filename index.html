Built by beekeepers, stocked for beekeepers.

BEE TECH started as a two-hive backyard operation outside Kampala. When local suppliers ran short on quality frames and reliable smokers, we started importing and building our own — then opened the shelves to every apiary in the region.

Today we run twelve partner apiaries, inspect every hive component before it ships, and bottle honey the same week it's harvested.

Field Notes

Beginner beekeeping tips

Short, practical notes pulled from our own inspection logs.

Inspection

Inspect every 7–10 days

Check brood pattern, queen activity, and stores without opening the hive so often that you stress the colony.

Smoker Use

Cool smoke, short puffs

Thick white smoke in short bursts calms bees without overheating the hive. Never use hot, thin smoke.

Space

Add supers before they're needed

A crowded brood box triggers swarming. Give the colony room to expand one step ahead of the nectar flow.

Protection

Suit up in light colors

Bees respond defensively to dark, fuzzy textures — light, smooth fabric keeps inspections calmer for everyone.
